Markwright

This is the basic interface of Markwright. It consists of three adjustable panes, two on the left and one on the right. The panes on the left are for editing your content, styles and metadata and reviewing issues, while the pane on the right serves as a live preview of your document.

These panes are swappable and adjustable, so if you find yourself needing additional room on either side of the screen, you can change the relative sizes accordingly.

Save your blank, newly-created document with File → Save As.

Store it in a directory of your choice. Note that content imported into your project will be imported relative to this directory, so creating a new directory for the project and storing all your document resources (content, images, fonts, styles, etc.) there isn't a bad idea.

The editor

The editor consists of three tabs, one for each mode of document creation — content, styles and metadata.

Content

"Content" is text that defines the main structure of the document. This content is written in Markwright-Flavored Markdown and is initially agnostic to the theme of your document. This theoretically allows you to rapidly cycle content through a variety of themes without having to make significant changes to your text.

Images and transcluded Markdown files can be imported into the document using their respective syntaxes, which are covered more in the article on MFM.

Crucially, these imports do not embed the content into your Markwright project. Adding an image to your document won't copy, mangle and store it as a binary blob in the middle of your document — it will simply create a link to it that Markwright invokes on viewing or exporting.

Similarly, any number of Markdown documents can be transcluded via HTTPS or file-system access. The contents will not be stored permanently in your project, but are instead imported at build-time.

It's best to keep your content files small and to-the-point, and keep your longform content stored in your project directory. Markwright is not well-suited as a text editor.

Styles

"Styles" is text that dictates the presentation of your document. In Markwright, styles are written in SCSS, a stylesheet preprocessor that provides a number of helpful features to enable clean, compact code.

Metadata

"Metadata" is data about your document's structure and pagination. It does not dictate the visual aspects of your layouts, not does it dictate the content within your document.

It stores information and directives used by Markwright to process and generate your document.

Metadata is written in JSONC, a superset of JSON that allows for the addition of inline and block-level comments. Details about the specifics of document configuration via metadata are available in the documentation on Metadata.

Problems

The problems pane displays issues detected by Markwright in your content, styles and metadata. These include broken image links, transclusions, syntax errors, etc.

Preview

The preview pane shows you a live preview of your content, as modified by your styles. Hold down the Ctrl key and hover over an element to activate a tooltip showing the element name and classes.
